Understand

  • Millennial advisory councils
  • Quantitative research
  • Youth trend analysis

Communicate

  • Marketing strategy
  • Campaign creation
  • Digital and SMS strategy

Activate

  • Full campaign execution
  • Testing and campaign optimization
  • Grants and scholarship programs

About

Meet the team

  • Twitter LinkedIn
    Aria Finger
    President, TMI; CEO, DoSomething.org

    Aria has a decade of experience motivating young people to take action through DoSomething.org campaigns such as Teens for Jeans, Thumb Wars, and Comeback Clothes. She has created brand partnerships with H&M, Coca-Cola, Sprint, JetBlue, and Toyota, and she has helped scale DoSomething.org to 4.9 million members in 125 countries today. Aria is one of the first ten World Economic Forum Global Shapers, she’s passionate about social justice, and she thinks the name “Aria” is becoming way too popular.

  • Twitter LinkedIn
    Meredith Ferguson
    Director

    Meredith's passion for creativity and innovation leads her to challenge traditional thinking and deliver the very best work for TMI's clients. Her career has spanned both client and agency roles. Prior to joining TMI, Meredith created ILostABet.org and led client work with Sam's Club, White House Black Market, Dirt Devil, and the Cleveland Clinic Foundation. Meredith is a graduate of Vanderbilt Law School and loves exploring every nook and cranny of New York City with her husband and two girls.

  • Twitter LinkedIn
    Lisa Boyd
    Lead Strategist

    Lisa has been with TMI since day one and has led strategy for many of the agency's largest clients. She’s an expert in all things young people—from messaging to user experience to SMS/mobile strategy. Outside of TMI, Lisa is on the junior board of Education Through Music and is a volunteer counselor at Crisis Text Line. She thrives on live music, loves to hip hop dance, and speaks Swahili.

  • Twitter LinkedIn
    Greg Perlstein
    Director of Strategy & Partnerships

    Greg builds innovative, cross-sector partnerships that positively impact people and the planet. Prior to joining TMI, Greg managed strategic alliances at the not-for-profit First Book, where he partnered with Target, Teach For America, and Demand Media to deliver millions of new books to kids in need. Greg is a Michigan Ross MBA, Dance Marathon alum, and an all-heart, little-skill triathlete.

In her work with TMI clients, Maahika draws on her past experiences with Health Leads and the education initiative Providence Talks.
